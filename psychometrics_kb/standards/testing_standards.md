## Summary

Testing standards are best used as an organizing framework for evidence, not as a checklist of automatic pass/fail rules. A defensible test use connects validity, reliability/precision, fairness, operations, documentation, rights, and intended decisions.

## Checks

- Ask what score interpretation and use the evidence is meant to support.
- Separate technical evidence from administrative, reporting, privacy, and rights issues.
- Treat new populations, languages, settings, or decisions as requiring renewed evidence.

## Use When

- The user asks whether an instrument is generally acceptable or standard-compliant.
- A report needs a high-level evidence map before specific analyses are chosen.
