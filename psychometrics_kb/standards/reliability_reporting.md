## Summary

Reliability evidence should describe the precision of scores under defined conditions. A single alpha coefficient is not enough when scores are interpreted at different ranges, used for decisions, or compared across groups.

## Checks

- Report the sample, form, administration mode, and score level tied to the estimate.
- Add standard errors, conditional precision, or decision consistency when individual decisions are made.
- Avoid using alpha as an absolute cutoff detached from construct breadth and intended use.

## Use When

- The user asks how to report reliability.
- The score will be interpreted for individuals, groups, bands, or classifications.
