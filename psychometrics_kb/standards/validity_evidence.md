## Summary

Validity is an argument about whether evidence supports a particular interpretation and use of scores. Internal structure is only one part of that argument; content relevance, response processes, relations to other variables, consequences, and sample/settings also matter.

## Checks

- State the intended score use before recommending statistical models.
- Link CFA, IRT, reliability, and DIF results back to the construct definition.
- Warn when a user treats one coefficient or one fit index as proof of validity.

## Use When

- The user asks whether a scale is valid.
- The intended use is vague, new, or broader than the evidence supplied.
