## Summary

Group comparisons require evidence that the construct is measured comparably. Configural, metric, scalar, and sometimes strict invariance checks create a decision trail before comparing latent means or observed scores.

## Checks

- Require an explicit grouping variable and adequate group sizes.
- Check configural fit before constraining loadings or thresholds/intercepts.
- If full invariance fails, consider partial invariance with transparent item-level justification.

## Use When

- The user wants to compare groups.
- The study includes gender, language, site, culture, disability, or demographic subgroup comparisons.
